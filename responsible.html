Responsible vs. Unethical AI Use

Use AI to learn faster, not to replace your work. These examples make it clear what helps you grow and what crosses the line.

Listen now Practice the skills
Responsible AI Use

Good learning habits

  • Getting feedback on a personal statement and revising it yourself.
  • Asking AI what is wrong with your code, then debugging the issue yourself.
  • Learning new concepts and asking follow-up questions.
  • Creating practice questions to study for a midterm.
  • Practicing interview questions and answers.
Unethical AI Use

Shortcuts that hurt you

  • Having AI write your entire statement of purpose.
  • Copying AI code word-for-word and not understanding what it did.
  • Not learning course content and having AI do all the work.
  • Using an AI-generated response as your own content.
  • Using AI tools during exams or quizzes without permission.
Always follow your school or workplace AI policies. If you are unsure, ask your instructor, supervisor, or academic advisor, or program guidelines before using AI for graded or paid work.

Campus Survey Snapshot

Method: campus convenience survey (n=12), collected in Winter 2026. This is a small sample and reflects local perceptions.

What students think about AI

  • 75% rated AI at 5/10 for overall positivity (mostly neutral, slightly negative-leaning).
  • Most respondents rated college students' ethical AI use very low (mainly 1-2 on a 1-10 scale).
  • Most respondents learned AI from external sources (social media, friends, online articles, and self-teaching), not formal instruction.

Key themes from responses

  • Top support needs: job applications, interview practice, and time management.
  • Concerns: cheating, overreliance, reduced independent thinking, and less use of office hours.
  • Balanced views: AI can be useful as a supplement when used responsibly.
  • Benefits: breaking down difficult content and supporting personalized learning.
Why this matters: These responses reinforce our focus on responsible AI use. AI should support learning, not replace critical thinking, honest effort, or instructor support.

Student Voice (selected quotes)

  • "AI can help with self-teaching, but I sometimes skip office hours even when a TA or professor would explain it better."
  • "I have mostly seen AI used to cheat on assignments, and it can become a substitute for real learning."
  • "AI can either enhance learning or inhibit learning, depending on how it is used."
  • "AI is helpful for breaking down difficult content into simpler steps."
