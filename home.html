Why AI skills matter now

AI is quickly becoming a basic academic and career skill. The real advantage is not building AI yourself, but knowing how to question it, verify it, and use it responsibly. Those habits now shape how students study, think, and prepare for work.

Smarter studying

Turn long readings into quick notes and practice questions you can revisit fast.

86% of students globally are regularly using AI in their studies.
Source: Digital Education Council

Sharper thinking

AI can speed up brainstorming, but you still need judgment to choose what is accurate and useful.

70% of the skills used in most jobs today are expected to change by 2030, with AI as a catalyst.
Source: LinkedIn Work Change Report 2025

Future-ready

Graduate programs and employers increasingly expect responsible, practical AI fluency.

3x+ growth in U.S. AI-related job postings since 2016.
Source: Stanford HAI AI Index

Dr Young's Podcast - Young & Hungry

The Young & Hungry Podcast explores how artificial intelligence is shaping the future of education and work. Through conversations with experts and innovators, the podcast helps young and hungry college students understand responsible AI use and how developing AI skills can support career exploration. Instead of fearing job loss from AI, students learn how to adapt, build new skills, and use AI as a tool for future opportunities.

001 - Using AI to guide assignments

Using AI to complete college assignments can be risky and laden with consequences. This is a guide for using AI to serve as a reference and framework for written assignments, and to dissuade students from plagiarism.

2

002 - Using AI to teach yourself and other people

Another use for AI is to use it to study for upcoming tests and exams. However, it is important to always cross-reference the information given by AI with the curriculum taught in your professor's lectures.

3

003 - Creative digital media using AI

One of AI's weakest capabilities is in the creation of digital media. Most of the details provided in animation and art are incorrect and off-putting, meaning that authentic animation and digital media creation is still dominated by human-made artworks.
