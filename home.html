Why AI skills matter now

AI is quickly becoming a basic academic and career skill. The real advantage is not building AI yourself, but knowing how to question it, verify it, and use it responsibly. Those habits now shape how students study, think, and prepare for work.

Smarter studying

Turn long readings into quick notes and practice questions you can revisit fast.

86% of students globally are regularly using AI in their studies.
Source: Digital Education Council

Sharper thinking

AI can speed up brainstorming, but you still need judgment to choose what is accurate and useful.

70% of the skills used in most jobs today are expected to change by 2030, with AI as a catalyst.
Source: LinkedIn Work Change Report 2025

Future-ready

Graduate programs and employers increasingly expect responsible, practical AI fluency.

3x+ growth in U.S. AI-related job postings since 2016.
Source: Stanford HAI AI Index
